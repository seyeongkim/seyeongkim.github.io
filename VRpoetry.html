VR Poetry

2013 - 2015



In this project, we explore the next storytelling medium for poetry. A text poem is represented into a visual form of tree that reflects the characteristics of the poem. Poems of a traditional korean poet, Kim So-wol are reborn into VR space where anyone can walk around, see, listen, and touch the poem.

I took in charge of visualizing the computer graphic and designing the interaction between users and the virtual forest of poems in this project.

First Version - Nature Never, Chindallaekkot

6th International Conference on Intelligent Technologies for Interactive Entertainment (INTETAIN), Chicago, 2014

Second Version - Touching the Poem

3D Interactive Visualization and Navigation of Kim Sowol's Chindallaekkot (진달래 꽃 시집) poster, HCI Korea Creative Award, 2015
